## Introduction: who this guide is for

This scenario guide targets **renters and owners in studios and one-bedrooms** (roughly 25–55 m² / 270–590 sq ft) who want hands-off floor care without tripping over a bulky dock. You might run AC for hours during heat season, share space with a pet, or mix hardwood with a thin area rug — each layout stresses different parts of a robot vacuum: **suction at the brush roll, LiDAR vs camera mapping, mop pad hygiene, and how often you must empty the bin**.

We compare four mainstream models across four apartment scenarios. Reference prices reflect OEM and major US storefront listings as of {{today}}; KRW equivalents use {{usd_krw_rate}} (~{{krw:449}} for a mid-range auto-empty bundle, ~{{krw:29.99}} for a replacement filter pack).

## Scenario: Compact studio under 35 m² with narrow walkways

**What matters here**

- **Slim dock footprint** — some auto-empty bases need 40+ cm clearance behind furniture.
- **LiDAR mapping** — bump-and-turn robots miss corners in L-shaped studios.
- **Cliff and threshold handling** — bathroom lips and balcony tracks should not trap the unit.

**Recommended pick: Roborock Q8 Max+** (~$599 / ~{{krw:599}} with auto-empty dock)

Roborock publishes **5,500 Pa max suction** and Reactive Tech obstacle avoidance on the Q8 Max line, paired with PreciSense LiDAR mapping. The dock auto-empties into a bag rated for weeks in a solo studio, which matters when you are away during summer trips. Multi-floor maps help if your studio has a raised sleeping nook or a split-level entry.

**Runner-up:** Ecovacs Deebot N30 Pro Omni — strong mop station at a lower price, but the omni dock is wider; skip it if your only free wall is beside a bed.

| Factor | Roborock Q8 Max+ | Typical bump-and-turn budget unit |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Mapping | LiDAR, saved no-go zones | Random paths, missed strips |
| Dock size | Moderate auto-empty | Often bin-only, smaller |
| Suction (rated) | 5,500 Pa | 2,000–3,000 Pa common |
| Summer note | HEPA filter catches AC-circulated dust | More passes needed daily |

## Scenario: Pet hair on hardwood and low-pile rugs

**What matters here**

- **Brush roll design** — rubber fins vs bristle tangles on long hair.
- **Obstacle recognition** — summer means more shoes, leashes, and pet bowls on the floor.
- **Auto-empty frequency** — pet households fill bins in 1–2 runs without a dock.

**Recommended pick: iRobot Roomba Combo j5+** (~$499 / ~{{krw:499}} with Clean Base)

iRobot rates the j5 line for **pet hair pickup** with a multi-surface rubber brush and PrecisionVision obstacle avoidance (documented to steer around cords and pet waste alerts in app release notes). The j5+ bundle adds auto-empty — critical when fur clumps during shedding season overlapping summer AC months. Roomba’s app lets you draw keep-out zones around water bowls without remapping the whole unit.

**Runner-up:** Roborock Q8 Max+ — stronger raw suction on paper, but less proven obstacle AI for scattered toys during school break at home.

| Factor | Roomba Combo j5+ | Roborock Q8 Max+ |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Pet hair focus | Rubber brush, marketed pet pick | Strong suction, standard brush |
| Obstacle AI | PrecisionVision | LiDAR plus reactive avoidance |
| Mop | Combo pad (light wipe) | Optional separate mop arm on other SKUs |
| Empty cycle | Clean Base bag | RockDock bag |

## Scenario: Hard floors plus light mopping through humid summer weeks

**What matters here**

- **Mop lift on rugs** — wetting a thin entry mat causes odor fast in humidity.
- **Dock wash and dry cycle** — stagnant mop pads smell within days above 28 °C indoor dew point.
- **Water tank access** — tight kitchens make refilling awkward if the dock is wedged in a corner.

**Recommended pick: Dreame L10s Ultra** (~$699 / ~{{krw:699}})

Dreame documents **5,300 Pa suction** on the L10s Ultra with an all-in-one station that washes and hot-air dries mop pads — the maintenance task renters forget until smell appears. Mop lift is spec-listed for crossing carpets up to common pile heights. Pair with a dehumidifier strategy in the same room if you mop daily during monsoon-heavy weeks, and run the dock dry cycle after every session.

**Runner-up:** Ecovacs Deebot N30 Pro Omni — lower price with TruEdge mop extension, but pad drying takes longer without heated airflow on some bundles.

| Factor | Dreame L10s Ultra | Ecovacs Deebot N30 Pro Omni |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Pad care | Wash plus hot-air dry in dock | Wash plus air-dry in dock |
| Rated suction | 5,300 Pa | 8,000 Pa (Ecovacs marketing) |
| Price tier | Premium mid | Value omni |
| Humid-season risk | Lower if dry cycle runs | Moderate without heated dry |

## Scenario: Cluttered one-bedroom during summer break at home

**What matters here**

- **Zone cleaning** — kids and WFH gear shift daily; remapping should not require factory reset.
- **Noise at night** — late runs while AC fan is already audible should stay under ~65 dB.
- **Value per feature** — you may want mapping plus pad care without paying for a flagship omni station.

**Recommended pick: Ecovacs Deebot N30 Pro Omni** (~$549 / ~{{krw:549}})

Ecovacs advertises **LiDAR mapping with room zones**, 8,000 Pa rated suction on the N30 Pro line, and an omni station that empties dust and refreshes mop pads. It is the pragmatic pick when furniture layout changes weekly but you still want app-drawn no-go lines around play mats and desk chairs. The N30 Pro Omni undercuts Dreame and eufy flagship stations while keeping pad washing — useful when roommates rotate who empties the dock.

**Runner-up:** iRobot Roomba Combo j5+ — smarter obstacle handling, but higher cost if mopping is rarely used.

| Factor | Ecovacs Deebot N30 Pro Omni | Roomba Combo j5+ |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Mapping | LiDAR, zone edit | Smart maps plus vision |
| Dock | Self-empty plus pad wash | Clean Base only |
| Best clutter skill | Zones plus omni value | Obstacle alerts |
| Noise (typical spec) | ~65 dB max | ~60 dB max |

## Final Verdict

Apartment robot vacuums are not one-size-fits-all: **mapping tech and dock maintenance** separate smooth ownership from daily rescues. For most studio dwellers, **Roborock Q8 Max+** balances LiDAR accuracy and a tolerable dock size. Pet owners should prioritize **Roomba Combo j5+** brush and obstacle tooling. If you truly need mop automation through sticky summer humidity, budget for **Dreame L10s Ultra** pad washing and hot-air drying — skipping that step leads to the most common one-star complaints. **Ecovacs Deebot N30 Pro Omni** wins when clutter shifts often but you refuse to pay flagship omni-station prices.

| Buy if… | Skip if… |
| --- | --- |
| You want hands-off emptying in a small footprint | Your only floor space is thick shag carpet end-to-end |
| Hard floors dominate and AC runs daily (dust load) | You cannot reach the dock to change bags or filters monthly |
| You need zone cleans during school-break clutter | You expect deep mop replacement for sticky kitchen grime |
| Listed price fits after checking {{today}} promos | Landlord bans any wet cleaning on hardwood lease terms |

Check brush rolls and filters monthly during peak summer use — the best robot vacuum fails quietly when hair wraps the roll and suction collapses long before the battery dies. Replacement filters often cost around {{krw:29.99}} per pack; factor that into your first-year ownership math alongside dock bags.
