## Introduction: hot-swap is the 2026 entry-keyboard baseline

In the first half of 2026, entry mechanical keyboards normalized **hot-swap sockets** and **75% / TKL layouts** at lower prices. Buyers now compare **switch type (red/blue/brown)**, **keycap material (PBT vs ABS)**, and **internal foam** more than logo prestige.

This report ranks **five entry mechanical keyboards** using seller API metadata (price, switch specs, reviews, return rates). We focus on **verifiable specs** so first-time buyers avoid costly mismatch purchases.

**Key takeaway:** Keychron C3 for typing starters, Royal Kludge RK84 for wireless gaming value, Epomaker TH80 Pro for quiet offices, Redragon K552 for cheapest trial, Akko 5075B for customization growth.

## 1. Keychron C3 TKL

**Strengths:** PBT keycaps, hot-swap, Mac/Win caps included, balanced typing feel.

**Weaknesses:** Wired only, occasional uneven RGB, no wireless vs RK84.

**Verdict:** Safest **first board for typing and coding**.

## Five checks before you buy

1. **Switch type** — Red (linear), Blue (clicky), Brown (tactile). Avoid Blue in shared offices.
2. **Layout** — TKL drops numpad; 75% compresses F-row. Match your game and spreadsheet habits.
3. **Hot-swap** — Saves money if you plan to experiment with switches later.
4. **Keycap material** — PBT resists shine longer than ABS.
5. **Return rate** — Above 6% hints PCB/switch defects. All picks here except Redragon stay under 6%.

---

## Conclusion: prioritize switch, layout, and hot-swap over brand

Entry boards in 2026 differ most on **wireless support and hot-swap**, not logos. Keychron C3 is the safe typing starter, RK84 covers wireless gaming desks, and Epomaker TH80 Pro fits low-noise work.

If you are researching budget mechanical keyboards, decide **wired vs wireless and TKL vs 75%** first, then use the KBI table. Before checkout, try a switch sample pack to confirm feel.
