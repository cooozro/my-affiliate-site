## Why power bank specs matter in 2026

Phones and tablets now support **30W–45W USB-C PD**, but many cheap power banks still cap at 18W. That gap changes how fast you can recover battery during a commute or flight. This guide compares what actually matters: **real usable capacity**, output wattage, port count, weight, and certification marks.

## Key specs to compare

| Spec | What it means | Why it matters |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Rated mAh | Label capacity | Marketing number; real output is lower |
| USB-C PD wattage | Max charging speed | 30W+ for modern phones |
| Weight | Portability | 10,000mAh units should stay under ~220g |
| Certifications | UL / CE / PSE | Safety and airline travel confidence |

## Buying checklist

1. **Match PD wattage to your phone** — check your charger brick label.
2. **Prefer USB-C in/out** — micro-USB only models are outdated.
3. **Check airline limits** — usually 100Wh (roughly 27,000mAh at 3.7V) without approval.
4. **Avoid unknown brands** without certification logos.
5. **Read return rates** when available — high defect rates signal poor cells.

## Conclusion

In 2026, a good budget power bank is not just “big mAh.” Look for **30W PD, USB-C, and certified cells** in a weight you will actually carry. Match tier to trip length, not marketing numbers alone.
