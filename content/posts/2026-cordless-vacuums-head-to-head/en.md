## Introduction: why these three models for summer floor care

A typical July week in a one-bedroom apartment looks like this: the window AC runs from noon to midnight, the front door opens for deliveries and kids on break, and fine dust collects on hardwood while bedroom carpet traps pet hair. A robot vacuum handles daily crumbs, but edges, stairs, and couch cushions still need a stick vac you can grab between meetings.

This head-to-head pits **three widely stocked 2026 cordless stick models** — Dyson V15 Detect, Samsung Bespoke Jet AI, and Shark Stratos Cordless (IZ862H) — on the axes buyers actually feel: **rated air watts (AW), real-world runtime on carpet vs eco, whole-machine HEPA claims, and pickup when you alternate hardwood hallways with medium-pile rugs**. Reference prices reflect OEM and major US storefront listings in July 2026 (~$749 / ₩990,000 for the Dyson, ~$699 / ₩920,000 for Samsung, ~$499 / ₩660,000 for Shark).

**Key takeaway:** Dyson V15 Detect leads laser-guided hard-floor dust visibility and published AW transparency; Samsung Jet AI pushes peak AW with AI load sensing; Shark Stratos wins runtime-per-dollar and carpet-focused brush design when HEPA sealing matters on a tighter budget.

## 1. Dyson V15 Detect

**Strengths:** Dyson publishes **240 AW maximum suction** — rare transparency in this category — and pairs it with a laser slim-fluffy head that makes fine dust visible on dark hardwood. Whole-machine **HEPA filtration** is documented for sealed allergen capture when your AC recirculates indoor air all day. The piezo sensor counts and sizes particles, which helps you know when summer sand and pollen loads are high. Swappable batteries exist in some bundles, extending school-break cleaning marathons.

**Weaknesses:** At ~$749, it is the priciest entry here; boost mode drains faster on thick carpet, and some buyers report **15–25 minute effective runtime** on high pile when the high-torque head is engaged. Dust bins are smaller than Shark’s XL path — more empty stops during whole-apartment sessions. Replacement filters and tools add ownership cost over years.

**Analysis takeaway:** For most buyers with **mostly hard floors, visible dust, and allergy-sensitive households**, the V15 Detect is the precision pick — you trade money for spec clarity and laser-guided hard-floor confidence.

**Verdict:** Best **hard-floor visibility and published AW transparency** when budget allows.

---

## 2. Samsung Bespoke Jet AI

**Strengths:** Samsung lists up to **280 AW in max mode** — the highest published figure in this trio — with **AI Sensing** that raises suction on carpet transitions and eases off on bare floors to preserve battery. The lightweight stick body suits quick pickup between video calls during work-from-home heat waves. Washable fine-dust filters and multi-layer filtration support HEPA-class capture when maintained on schedule. Optional Clean Station docks (sold separately) reduce empty-bin dust clouds — relevant if you empty indoors with AC running.

**Weaknesses:** **~40 minutes in standard mode** trails Shark’s eco claims; max AW bursts shorten sessions on whole-home cleans. AI behavior depends on firmware — early open reviews note occasional misreads on dark rugs. Fewer third-party accessories than Dyson; brush inventory varies by region.

**Analysis takeaway:** For most buyers who want **peak suction numbers and hands-off auto adjustment** across hardwood living rooms and bedroom carpet, Jet AI is the middle-price power play — provided you accept slightly shorter rated runtime than Shark.

**Verdict:** Best **peak AW with automatic floor sensing** for mixed layouts.

## 3. Shark Stratos Cordless (IZ862H)

**Strengths:** Shark targets **carpet-first buyers** with PowerFins HairPro brush rolls and **CleanSense IQ** that ramps motor speed when load increases — helpful for summer pet shedding on medium pile. **Anti-Allergen Complete Seal with HEPA filter** addresses recirculated dust during all-day cooling. Shark publishes up to **70 minutes in eco mode** — the longest headline runtime here — at the lowest MSRP (~$499). Flex wand and odor-neutralizer cartridge (model-dependent) suit couch and stair passes kids generate during break.

**Weaknesses:** Shark **does not publish air watts**, making direct AW shopping comparisons harder — you rely on brush design and third-party tests. Unit weight and top-heavy balance show up in long ceiling-corner sessions. Eco runtime drops sharply on carpet; realistic carpet sessions may land near **25–35 minutes**, not 70. Plastic bin latches are a recurring long-term review theme.

**Analysis takeaway:** For most buyers prioritizing **runtime per dollar and carpet hair pickup** over spec-sheet AW bragging, Stratos is the value anchor — especially in pet-friendly apartments with more rug than tile.

**Verdict:** Best **budget HEPA sealing and long eco runtime** for carpet-heavy homes.

## FAQ

### Does higher AW always mean better carpet pickup in summer?

Air watts measure suction power at the motor, but carpet results also depend on brush roll design, seal quality, and how long boost mode lasts before thermal or battery limits kick in. Samsung’s 280 AW max can dig into medium pile, yet runtime shrinks faster than Shark’s eco-first tuning. On hard floors, Dyson’s laser head often matters more than an extra 40 AW you cannot see. Match AW claims to your dominant floor type and realistic session length, not the biggest number on the box.

### My AC runs all day with windows closed — do I need whole-machine HEPA?

Closed-window cooling recirculates indoor air, so sealed filtration helps trap pollen, pet dander, and fine dust that sticks vacs kick up at the exhaust. Dyson documents whole-machine HEPA capture; Shark pairs HEPA with Anti-Allergen Complete Seal; Samsung relies on washable multi-layer filters you must maintain. Any of the three can work if filters are genuine and replaced on schedule — skip off-brand clones that break seals.

### Hardwood living room, carpet bedroom — one stick or two heads?

All three ship floor-specific heads or auto-sensing logic for transitions. Dyson’s laser slim-fluffy excels on hard floors; Samsung’s AI adjusts between rooms; Shark’s brush favors carpet load. A single stick with the right head swap beats buying a second cheap corded vac for edges. If your layout is mostly one surface type, optimize for that head rather than peak AW alone.
