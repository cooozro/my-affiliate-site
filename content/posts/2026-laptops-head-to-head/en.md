## 1. Lenovo IdeaPad Slim 3 15ABR8

**Strengths:** Ships with **16GB soldered** and a **Ryzen 5 7530U** that handles Chrome, Office, and video calls without immediate swap. Lenovo lists **300 nits** — the brightest panel here — which keeps PDFs readable near a dorm window without maxing brightness and draining the 47Wh pack. For summer break travel between home and campus, the balance of RAM and screen brightness reduces “why is everything gray?” moments during afternoon study sessions.

**Weaknesses:** RAM is **not upgradeable** after purchase. Plastic chassis flexes, and public reviews note **audible fan ramps** when room temperatures climb above 30°C without air conditioning. At $499 (~{{krw:499}}), it is the priciest of the three — you pay for convenience, not DIY headroom.

**Analysis takeaway:** For most buyers, this translates to **opening twenty browser tabs without swap panic** and **the least washed-out screen beside sunny windows** — if you accept soldered memory and occasional fan noise in heat.

**Verdict:** Best **16GB-plus-300-nit out-of-the-box** pick when upgrade slots do not matter.

## 2. Acer Aspire 5 A515-58

**Strengths:** **Core i5-1335U** solid for spreadsheets and light editing. The standout is an **accessible SODIMM slot** — add an 8GB stick after back-to-school sales to reach 16GB cheaply. **50Wh battery** is the largest here, helping library marathons when outlets are scarce. Full-size keyboard with numeric pad suits accounting and data-entry coursework.

**Weaknesses:** Ships with **8GB only** — heavy multitasking feels sluggish until you upgrade. **250 nits** struggles beside bright windows and on shaded outdoor benches. At 1.76 kg, it is the heaviest carry of the trio. Plain chassis, no premium feel.

**Analysis takeaway:** For most buyers who can **budget one RAM stick within a month**, this is the **lowest-friction upgrade path** — but not the pick if you need 16GB on day one in a hot, bright dorm.

**Verdict:** Best **upgrade-friendly budget** when you accept 8GB at checkout.

## 3. ASUS Vivobook Go 15 E1504FA

**Strengths:** **Lowest list price** at about $429 (~{{krw:429}}) with **16GB soldered** and a lightweight 1.63 kg body — ideal for summer trips between home and campus. **Ryzen 5 7520U** is efficient for notes, streaming, and light coding. You skip the Aspire’s “buy RAM later” step entirely.

**Weaknesses:** **42Wh battery** is the smallest — plan a **PD power bank** for all-day library sessions without outlets. **250 nits** and **soldered RAM** mirror the IdeaPad’s limitations on brightness and upgrades, but without the Slim 3’s 300-nit advantage. Fan noise under combined heat and compile loads appears in public reviews.

**Analysis takeaway:** For most buyers who want **the cheapest 16GB laptop today** and can charge midday, this is the travel-friendly compromise — not the endurance leader.

**Verdict:** Best **lowest-price 16GB** pick when weight beats maximum runtime.

## FAQ

### My dorm has no AC during heat waves — which fan noise is least annoying?

None of these are silent ultrabooks under load, but weight and cooling design differ. The **ASUS Vivobook Go 15** is lightest at 1.63 kg, so heat-soak in a small uncooled room can spike fans sooner during video calls plus twenty tabs. The **Lenovo IdeaPad Slim 3** runs a stronger Ryzen 5 7530U with 16GB headroom, which reduces swap-driven fan spikes but can still ramp audibly above 30°C — public reviews mention this often. The **Acer Aspire 5** at 8GB may swap heavily before you add RAM, which keeps fans busy in heat. If you cannot add AC, prioritize **16GB today** and study near shaded spots; a small desk fan helps more than chasing a “silent” budget chassis.

### I study beside a west-facing window — do 250 nits vs 300 nits really matter?

Indoor desk work often tolerates **250 nits**, but summer sun through a dorm window washes out contrast fast. Lenovo’s **300-nit** Slim 3 panel is the only model here with meaningful extra headroom — you can keep brightness at 70–80% instead of 100% and still read scanned PDFs. Both the Aspire and Vivobook list **250 nits**, which means max brightness and shorter effective battery on glare-heavy afternoons. None match phone OLED outdoors; for frequent patio or quad seating, pick the brightest spec you can afford and pair with a matte anti-glare screen protector if available.

### Should I buy 16GB upfront or get 8GB plus a stick later on the Acer?

**Upfront 16GB** saves hassle when RAM is soldered — as on the IdeaPad and Vivobook. If you choose the **Acer Aspire 5**, verify the service manual for **single vs dual-channel** layout before buying a module; 8GB plus 8GB dual-channel often beats awkward single-channel layouts for integrated graphics. Summer back-to-school sales are a practical window to add RAM and an external SSD together. Waiting until swap becomes unbearable mid-semester costs time during finals. If you cannot add a stick within four weeks, the IdeaPad or Vivobook’s soldered 16GB is the safer default.
