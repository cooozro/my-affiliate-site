## Introduction: window AC when the forecast stays above 90°F

In {{today}}, renters and studio dwellers facing **tiered summer electricity rates** return to the same hardware question: which **window-mounted unit** cools a 12–18 m² bedroom or one-room apartment without blowing the monthly bill or blocking the only operable window. Window AC still delivers **more cooling per watt** than single-hose portables when installation is allowed — but not every 8,000 BTU box fits the same sill width, and noise at low fan speed matters when you sleep with the unit two meters from the pillow.

Since 2020, the US market has split into three lanes: **classic fixed-chassis boxes** (Frigidaire, GE), **U-shaped inverter units** that straddle the sash (Midea MAW08), and **compact quiet 6,000 BTU designs** for tight bedrooms (Haier Serenity). Cross-shopping requires reading whether CEER is measured at AHAM conditions, whether dB is at low or high fan, and whether “smart” features add standby watts you pay for all season.

This report compares **five mainstream window AC models** suited to small apartments and heat-season bedrooms. We cross-check public manufacturer spec sheets, Energy Star listings where available, and open reviews. The focus is **verifiable BTU, CEER, sleep-speed noise, estimated daily electricity, and install footprint** — not proprietary commerce data.

**Key takeaway:** For a standard double-hung window in a 15 m² bedroom, Midea MAW08V1QWT leads on CEER and low-speed noise; Frigidaire FFRE083WAE wins upfront cost; LG LW8017ERSM fits buyers who want ThinQ scheduling; Haier ESAQ406P suits sub-250 sq ft rooms that prioritize quiet; GE AHY08LZ balances EZ Mount install with Wi-Fi at a mid-tier CEER.

We compute a **Small-Room Window Index (SRWI)** per model: higher CEER for the price tier, lower claimed dB at low fan, lower rated watts at 8,000 BTU class, and useful inverter or smart scheduling raise the score. Daily kWh assumes **8 hours of mixed cooling** at rated wattage and **$0.15/kWh** — adjust for your utility rate and local heat index.

## 1. Midea MAW08V1QWT U-Shaped Smart Inverter

**Strengths:** Highest CEER in the group (15.0 per Energy Star listing), inverter compressor smooths temperature swings, U-shaped mount keeps partial window view, 42 dB sound power at low fan per OEM manual (ISO 3744), Wi-Fi and voice control, 8,000 BTU rated for 350 sq ft.

**Weaknesses:** Heaviest install in the set — bracket alignment takes two people; minimum window width 22 in per spec sheet; premium price near {{krw:379}}; R32 refrigerant requires upright shipping and careful unboxing.

**Analysis takeaway:** For most buyers with a **compatible double-hung window**, this translates to the **lowest estimated annual kWh in the 8,000 BTU class here** while preserving daylight — the right pick when the lease allows a straddle bracket and you run AC through humid August nights.

**Verdict:** Best **efficiency and sleep noise** pick when U-shaped install is viable.

## 2. Frigidaire FFRE083WAE

**Strengths:** Lowest upfront price in the comparison (~{{krw:249}}), straightforward fixed-chassis install, Energy Star certified, 8,000 BTU / 350 sq ft AHAM rating, mechanical-friendly electronic controls, widely stocked during heat-season crunches.

**Weaknesses:** 55 dB at low fan is audible in silent bedrooms; CEER 12 trails Midea by a wide margin; 197 CFM high airflow is adequate but not exceptional; no app scheduling without a separate smart plug.

**Analysis takeaway:** For most buyers, this translates to **fastest path to cold air on a budget** — acceptable for renters who may remove the unit each autumn and want the smallest checkout hit before {{today}} heat advisories.

**Verdict:** Default **value fixed-chassis** option when noise above 50 dB at low speed is tolerable.

## 3. LG LW8017ERSM Smart Window Air Conditioner

**Strengths:** LG ThinQ app, Alexa and Google Assistant support, 660 W rated draw, CEER 12.0 / EER 12.1 per LG spec sheet, 220 CFM airflow, 2.2 pt/hr dehumidification, 4-way air deflection, auto restart after power blips.

**Analysis takeaway:** For most buyers, this translates to **scheduling cool-down before you arrive home from commute** without walking to the window — set Eco mode from the app during tiered-rate afternoon blocks.

**Verdict:** Top pick for **app-first buyers** who want 8,000 BTU with familiar LG smart-home hooks.

## 4. Haier ESAQ406P Serenity

**Strengths:** Quietest low-speed claim in the table (~43 dB per Haier documentation), compact footprint for narrow sills, 6,050 BTU / 250 sq ft fit prevents oversizing in 10–12 m² bedrooms, 490 W rated draw yields lowest daily cost estimate here, cross-flow fan design reduces turbulence whine.

**Weaknesses:** Not enough BTU for combined living/sleep open plans above ~23 m²; CEER 11.0 and EER 11.2 trail 8,000 BTU peers; wider minimum window width (26 in) surprises some buyers; no Wi-Fi on this Serenity SKU.

**Analysis takeaway:** For most buyers, this translates to **sleep-first cooling in a small bedroom** where an 8,000 BTU unit would short-cycle — you trade peak capacity for steady, low-noise nights during heat waves.

**Verdict:** Best **compact quiet bedroom** pick under 250 sq ft — not for studio all-day cooling.

## Five checks before you buy

1. **Size BTU to measured floor area** — 8,000 BTU suits many 12–18 m² rooms; a 10 m² bedroom may run better on 6,000 BTU (Haier) to avoid short cycling. Measure length × width before trusting “up to 350 sq ft” marketing.
2. **Measure the window before the unit** — U-shaped Midea needs 22 in minimum width; Haier Serenity needs 26 in. Casement and slider windows often need adapter kits not included in these SKUs.
3. **Read dB at the fan speed you will sleep with** — A 57 dB low setting (GE) feels different than 42 dB (Midea manual) when the grille faces the bed. Prioritize low-speed noise over max BTU if the unit sits on the sleep wall.
4. **Compare CEER, not BTU alone** — Two 8,000 BTU boxes can differ by 25% in estimated annual kWh. CEER 15 (Midea) vs 11.4 (GE) compounds over a four-month heat season at {{usd_krw_rate}} exchange-sensitive power bills.
5. **Plan seasonal removal and storage** — Fixed-chassis units weigh 50–58 lb. Confirm building rules, bracket hardware, and whether you need a second person for U-shaped bracket alignment before {{today}} delivery windows close.
