## 1. Aquasana AQ-5300+ Max Flow Under-Sink Filter

**Strengths:** Highest SHI here on gallon-per-dollar filter life (600 gallons per Claryum block per Aquasana spec), dedicated brushed-nickel faucet included on most kits, NSF 42/53/401/P473 certifications for lead, PFAS-class reduction claims where listed, 0.72 GPM max flow suits filling pitchers during heat waves, compact 9-inch under-sink footprint frees counter for summer meal prep.

**Weaknesses:** Requires cabinet drill for faucet hole — renters may need landlord approval; no TDS demolition like RO — dissolved mineral taste may persist in hard-water cities; professional install adds $150–$300 if you skip DIY; filter replacement needs shutoff confidence under the sink.

**Editorial read:** 600-gallon cartridges at about $160/year land near **$679 three-year TCO** (unit plus filters) for a four-person July bottled-water swap; 0.72 GPM cuts pitcher wait time. Nine-inch under-sink clearance frees **summer prep counter space**.

**Review concern:** No RO-level TDS drop leaves **chalky ice and mineral taste** in hard-water cities; renter drill and install add **move-out restore cost**.

**Analysis takeaway:** I’d call this **on-demand cooking and bottle water from a second faucet** — no tower blocking the fridge, and about **$160/year** in filters usually beats July bottled-water runs for four people.

**Verdict:** Best **under-sink value** pick when you own the kitchen and want NSF-listed carbon performance without RO waste water.

## FAQ
### Countertop or under-sink — which wins for summer hydration volume?

**Under-sink (Aquasana AQ-5300+)** wins when you own the cabinet and fill large pitchers ten times a week — flow stays on tap without counter clutter. **Countertop (Brita Hub, Coway, Bluewater)** wins when drilling is forbidden or you want RO TDS drop without a plumber. For light solo hydration, **PUR FM-3700B** avoids both footprint and install entirely.

### Does a carbon filter lower TDS on a handheld meter?

Usually **not much** — carbon targets chlorine, lead class contaminants, and taste. Your TDS pen may still read 150–300 ppm on municipal water after PUR or Brita. **RO units (Coway, Bluewater)** push readings toward bottled-water bands because dissolved minerals are rejected. Do not buy carbon expecting desalination; match technology to your water report.

### How much should I budget for filters during a hot summer?

Assume **2× winter water consumption** June–August. PUR FM-3700B at 100 gallons per cartridge may need swaps every 10–12 weeks for a family of four (about $100/year). Aquasana’s 600-gallon block often lasts the rated six months unless you irrigate houseplants from the filtered faucet. RO composites on Coway and Bluewater stretch toward **annual swaps** — factor $90–$120 on top of electricity for the pump.

### Is professional install worth it for under-sink filters?

Aquasana AQ-5300+ is DIY-friendly if you can drill a 1.25-inch faucet hole and place the pressure valve — budget **$0–$50** in parts. Licensed plumbers charge **$150–$300** in many US metros for the same job, worthwhile if granite, disposal plumbing, or tight cabinet access intimidates you. Countertop and faucet mounts should stay DIY to preserve warranties.

### Hydration during heat waves — does filtered water beat bottled?

Filtered tap reduces **plastic waste and trunk lifting** during 95°F weeks when stores sell out of cases. Taste preference after RO (Coway, Bluewater) often matches mid-tier bottled; carbon-only (PUR, Brita, Aquasana) matches good municipal tap with chlorine removed. Keep reusable bottles cold in the fridge — no purifier replaces **actual drinking habit** when kids are outdoors all day.

## Five checks before you buy

1. **Read your municipal water quality report first** — If chlorine taste is the only complaint, carbon (PUR, Brita, Aquasana) suffices. If hardness or TDS exceeds local guidance, weigh Coway or Bluewater RO before chasing fancier carbon stages.
2. **Measure counter depth with dishwasher door open** — Countertop RO towers need 14–18 inches clear depth; Brita Hub needs faucet hose reach. Under-sink frees counter but steals cabinet volume beside the disposal.
3. **Calculate filter dollars, not just unit MSRP** — PUR’s $45 faucet mount can exceed Aquasana’s $199 system over three years if you ignore 100-gallon swap cycles during heat season.
4. **Confirm faucet compatibility before checkout** — Pull-down sprayers, square designer spouts, and low-flow aerators often reject PUR click mounts; Brita Hub adapters fail on some European faucet shapes per owner forums.
5. **Plan waste water for RO** — Coway and Bluewater need a drain clip or bowl route; summer guests mean more reject water — unacceptable if you cannot route to the sink basin.
